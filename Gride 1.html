What is IOT?

The Internet of Things (IoT) is a network of physical objects that are connected to the internet and can communicate with each other and with other devices. IoT devices, also known as "smart objects“.

Examples of IOT:

  • Connected cars.
  • Smart appliances.
  • Connected security systems.
  • Smart agriculture equipment.
  • Connected retail.
  • Connected healthcare monitors.
  • Connected manufacturing equipment.
  • Connected cities.

Applications of IOT:

1.Smart Home Applications:

Smart homes are a key IoT application. Examples include Mark Zuckerberg's AI home automation and Alan Pan's system using musical notes for in-house functions.

2.Wearables:

Wearable technology, an early adopter of IoT, includes devices like Fitbits, heart rate monitors, and smartwatches. The Guardian glucose monitoring device helps diabetics by detecting glucose levels with a sensor under the skin and transmitting data via radiofrequency.

3.Hacked Car:

Connected cars use Internet and WAN networks, offering benefits like infotainment, advanced navigation, and improved fuel efficiency.

4.Health care:

IoT transforms reactive medical systems into proactive wellness systems by enhancing device power, precision, and availability. IoT-enabled care devices collect real-world data to improve healthcare systems beyond just tools

Working with IoT Devices:

Collect and Transmit Data : 

Sensors are used in various applications to collect and transmit data based on specific requirements.

Actuate device based on triggers produced by sensors or processing devices:

Actuator devices perform actions when certain conditions or user-defined triggers are met.

Receive Information: 

Devices or users can receive information from the network for analysis and processing.

Communication Assistance:

This involves communication between two networks or IoT devices across the same or different networks. It is facilitated by protocols like MQTT, CoAP, ZigBee, FTP, and HTTP.

Advantages of IOT:

  • Improved efficiency and automation of tasks.
  • Increased convenience and accessibility of information.
  • Better monitoring and control of devices and systems.
  • Greater ability to gather and analyze data.
  • Improved decision-making.
  • Cost savings.

Disadvantages of IOT

  • Security concerns and potential for hacking or data breaches.
  • Privacy issues related to the collection and use of personal data.
  • Dependence on technology and potential for system failures.
  • Limited standardization and interoperability among devices.
  • Complexity and increased maintenance requirements.
  • High initial investment costs.
  • Limited battery life on some devices.
  • Concerns about job displacement due to automation.
